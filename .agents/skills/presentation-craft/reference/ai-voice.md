# AI-voice tells

The prose-slop layer in [slop.md](slop.md) catches generic filler. This catches a
narrower, sneakier thing: the analytical register a language model falls into when
it tries to sound insightful. A deck is communication, and these tells read as
machine-written to anyone with an ear for it. They hide in slide text and, more
often, in speaker notes. Scan for them as a third layer of the detector.

The fix is almost always the same: say the thing. Do not announce that you are
about to say it, reframe it, push back on it, or be honest about it. The reader
cannot see the setup and does not want it.

## The Claudism catalogue

Fifteen families. Each is a way of performing a thought instead of having one.

1. **Performative pushback** — announces a challenge it never delivers: "Let me push back", "the one place I'd push". *Fix:* state the disagreement and its reason, or cut it.
2. **The reframe announcement** — telegraphs a reframe instead of doing it: "Here's what's actually interesting", "the real question is", "Zoom out", "Let's step back". *Fix:* delete the signpost; lead with the reframed point.
3. **Naming the move** — meta-labels its own rhetoric: "The tell:", "the move here is", "what's load-bearing here". *Fix:* make the point without narrating that you are making it.
4. **Structural metaphor** — building and anatomy imagery worn to sound rigorous: "the structural spine", "load-bearing claim", "connective tissue", "the through-line", "heavy lifting". *Fix:* name the real dependency in plain words, or cut the metaphor.
5. **The antithesis flip** — a reversal that announces itself: "not just X but Y", "isn't X so much as Y", "X isn't the problem; Y is", "two things can be true at once". *Fix:* state the point straight.
6. **False candor and flattery** — "You're absolutely right", "Great question", "Let me be honest", "the honest answer is", plus validation stamps: "this is a real gap", "a genuine tension". *Fix:* drop the preamble; engage the substance.
7. **The aphoristic closer** — a punchy fragment for effect: "That's the whole game.", "And that changes everything.", "Simple as that." *Fix:* end on the substantive point; cut a closer that adds nothing.
8. **Emphasis and rhythm tics** — CAPS or bold mid-sentence, the compulsive rule of three, the colon-into-list reflex, clipped parallel negation ("Search ships now. Saved views do not."). *Fix:* let the sentence carry the weight; vary list length; break the triads.
9. **The false universal** — inflates one point into a law: "the question every roadmap turns on", "every good strategy does this", "what every team eventually learns". *Fix:* make the claim about this situation and back it.
10. **The clean mental model** — hands over an invented decomposition as if canonical: "the cleanest way to think about this", "this splits neatly into two", "there are basically three kinds of". *Fix:* keep a split only if it changes what the audience does.
11. **The strategy-memo register** — compressed memo confidence that skips the evidence: "two vendors prove the model and show the gap", "where the puck is going", "a tension to hold honestly", "the move here is". *Fix:* name the real capability, the real gap with its evidence, and the real reason.
12. **The bolt-on significance sentence** — a free-floating claim of larger importance: "there's a bigger story here", "this connects to a broader pattern", "zooming out, this matters because". *Test:* delete it; if nothing is lost, it was bolted on.
13. **The colon reveal** — a colon manufacturing suspense before a one-clause payoff: "Here's the catch: it games easily", "The problem: nobody owns it". *Fix:* write the full sentence.
14. **The conditions checklist** — requirements dressed as formal necessities: "for this to work, four things have to hold", "the invariant here is". *Fix:* list the actual requirements and how you would know each is met; drop the tidy number.
15. **The over-corrected anti-AI register** — prose straining not to read as AI, which is its own tell: staccato three-word fragments on every beat, forced lowercase, a "look," or "real talk" cold open bolted onto formal content, every natural dash contorted into an ellipsis, a planted typo. *Fix:* real voice chosen on purpose; vary length for real, long sentences included; a dash becomes a comma or full stop in a sentence you would actually write.

## The vocabulary watchlist

Two clusters. A few are unavoidable; a pile-up is the flag. Replace with plain English.

- **Thesaurus reach:** delve, tapestry, landscape, realm, beacon, nuanced, multifaceted, robust, meticulous, pivotal, underscore, intricate, interplay, garner, foster, bolster, harness, illuminate, facilitate, streamline, showcase, vibrant, leverage, seamless.
- **Analytical register:** load-bearing, tractable, crisp, surface (as a verb), gestures at, in tension with, orthogonal, first-order, downstream, non-trivial, the crux, fundamentally, the interesting tension.
- **Overused qualifiers:** arguably, it's worth noting, importantly, notably, furthermore, consequently, ultimately.
- **Verb inflation:** "serves as", "stands as", "represents", "marks" where "is" would do.

## Assistant-artifact slop (absolute)

Leftover chat scaffolding. Like the em dash, one instance anywhere fails the draft — a deck does not negotiate with its own reader.

- **Trailing offers:** "Would you like me to draft the next slide?", "Let me know if you'd like me to expand this", "Is there anything else you'd like me to cover?"
- **Form-letter sign-offs:** "I hope this helps", "I hope this finds you well".
- **Model self-reference:** "as a large language model", "as an AI", "based on my knowledge cutoff", "I don't have personal opinions".

*Fix:* delete every trace of the assistant turn. A deck ends on its last real point; it does not offer to continue the conversation.

## Uniform rhythm

No keyword catches this and no vocabulary swap fixes it, so it survives a scrub that removes every flagged word. It is the tell an ear catches first. The check is mechanical: read the notes aloud, or count. If three sentences in a row land within a few words of each other, or every paragraph runs the same three-to-four-sentence size, the meter is machine-even. *Fix:* vary length on purpose. Let one sentence run long and the next be three words. Break any paragraph that matches its neighbours in size.
